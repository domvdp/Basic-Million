This is perhaps the best place to start your journey towards financial freedom. It is going to take discipline, and so this MINDSET section will have you gathering an understanding of why you genuinely want more money, and once you've discovered a meaningful reason, it will keep you from questioning whether or not it is all worth the trouble; it will keep you from quitting.

For the vast majority, I can assure you that aiming to buy an expensive car or house will not lead to financial freedom, and so you'll want to think a little harder about your goal. The good news is, I will be providing you with all the information necessary to understand yourself better, and therefore helping you identify your personal reasons for obtaining more wealth.

We are also going to discuss how you can increase your happiness by simply changing your persective. You are likely more unhappy when you compare yourself to those who have more, which in turn leads to an everlasting cycle of spending more money in attempt to feel less inferior. Try not to compare yourself to those who 'appear' to have it better than you, because there will always be someone with more, and you'll never feel like you have enough. So, let's start by comparing yourself to those who have it much worse, and trust me, there is always someone who has it worse. If you do this, you'll learn to appreciate what you have, feel more satisfied, spend less money and ulimately have 'more' for the rest of your life.

Your mind is far more powerful than you know.
